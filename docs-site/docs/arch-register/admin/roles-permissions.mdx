Permissions in Arch Register are layered rather than flat.

## Permission Layers

The main layers are:

- global permissions
- workspace roles
- team roles

## Workspace Roles

Workspace roles govern capabilities across the workspace, including entity- and workspace-scoped content.

Typical concerns include:

- access to settings
- member and team administration
- schema editing
- audit visibility
- viewing and editing entity/workspace content

The dedicated content capabilities are:

- `content.view` — view and download entity- and workspace-scoped content
- `content.edit` — create, update, move, and delete entity- and workspace-scoped content

By default, every built-in workspace role can view content. Owners, administrators, and editors can also edit it.
These capabilities do not control project-scoped content; projects retain their project and owner-team authorization.

## Team Roles

Team roles govern access to owned entities and projects. Project content follows project authorization, while content
attached directly to an entity follows the workspace role's `content.view` and `content.edit` capabilities.

## Global Permissions

Global permissions sit outside one workspace and cover platform-wide administration such as global role assignment.
