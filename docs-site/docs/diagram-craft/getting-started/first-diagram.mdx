# First Diagram

This walkthrough creates a small three-step diagram so you can learn the basic editing loop: add shapes, connect them, move them, and export the result.

## What You Are Looking At

The editor is built around three areas:

- the **shape picker** on the left for adding new elements
- the **canvas** in the center for arranging the diagram
- the **tool panels** around the canvas for editing structure and appearance

## Create A Simple Flow

1. Open a blank document or a sample document.
2. In the shape picker, drag a rectangle or rounded rectangle onto the canvas.
3. Add a second shape to the right of the first one.
4. Add a third shape below the second one.
5. Double-click each shape and enter short labels such as `Browser`, `API Service`, and `Database`.
6. Create a connector from the first shape to the second.
7. Create a second connector from the second shape to the third.

At this point you have the basic structure of a small flow diagram.

## Adjust The Layout

Use a few quick edits to make the diagram easier to read:

1. Drag shapes to give the connectors a clear path.
2. Resize shapes so their labels fit comfortably.
3. Keep related elements aligned horizontally or vertically where possible.
4. Zoom out if you need more space, then zoom back in to refine spacing.

You do not need a perfect layout on the first pass. The goal is to get comfortable moving between placement and refinement.

## What To Notice

As you work through the first diagram, pay attention to these behaviors:

- shapes snap into place as you move them
- connectors attach to anchor points on the shape edges
- text stays with its shape as you reposition the diagram
- the canvas lets you pan and zoom without changing the actual diagram content
