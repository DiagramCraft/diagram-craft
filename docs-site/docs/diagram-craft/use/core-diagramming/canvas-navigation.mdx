# Canvas Navigation

You should be able to zoom out to regain context, pan to the next area you want to refine, and then zoom back in without losing your place in the diagram.

## Start With Zoom And Pan

Most navigation in Diagram Craft comes down to two actions:

- zoom to change how much of the diagram you can see
- pan to move the current viewport without moving any diagram elements

Use zoom when the current level is wrong for the task. Zoom out to check the overall flow, then zoom in again when you need to place a connector precisely or edit a label.

Use pan when the scale is already right and you just need to move to another part of the canvas.

## Use Mouse Or Touchpad

If you are navigating directly on the canvas:

- scroll vertically or horizontally to pan across the canvas
- use `Ctrl` + scroll to zoom in or out around the pointer position
- hold `Space`, then click and drag to pan
- on a touchpad, use the platform gesture that maps to scroll for panning and the gesture that maps to `Ctrl` + scroll for zooming

This is the fastest way to move around a large diagram without switching tools.

## Use Keyboard Shortcuts

If you want precise, repeatable viewport control:

- `Command+=` zooms in
- `Command+-` zooms out
- `Command+0` zooms to fit

These are useful when you want to stay on the keyboard while editing or reviewing.
